Teammatic

design lead // UC San Diego Research 2016
tl;dr: I was in charge of designing a team formation UI tool for instructors called Teammatic. It features a clear, intuitive system for efficiently creating teams given a series of customizable constraints, i.e. availability, leadership, gender distribution.

The Problem

Creating good teams is hard. We found this especially so in the education system; as an instructor, the goal is to formulate efficient teams as soon as possible in order to progress through the material in a timely manner. Our initial findings found that instructors either 1) spent too much time trying to initially assess their students’ skills & qualities before organizing teams, or 2) randomly (or with little assessment) assigned teams that had varying levels of success.


We set out to create a tool that would make forming teams more effective and easy for instructors.

Our Process

Initial research went into trying to find existing tools that tried solving the problem of team formation. We found CATME (Comprehensive Assessment of Team Member Effectiveness) to be the only existing solution, which uses their Team-Maker tool to collect student information, and utilizes the data to assign teams according to a criteria specified by the instructor. However, user feedback has shown varying success within classrooms. Case study here.

Based on this and additional user interviews, our team established three design principles that would help guide our design decision throughout this process.


We wanted to build a tool that would:
  • Help instructors quickly form teams
  • Provide instructors with insightful feedback on team compatibility
  • Be simple and easy for instructors to use

The first thing we wanted to tackle was how instructors would actually form teams; we would focus on the onboarding process after. After a few paper-and-pen sketching sessions, we came up with a few ideas and drew them up as wireframes.
